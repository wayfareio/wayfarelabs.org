About

Wayfare Labs began as a question posed by Erwin McManus at the 2013 Q Los Angeles gathering:

What if the church became the human incubator for creating the world’s best future?

This question became the focal point of conversations for cofounders Brandon Arbini and Jeff Tanner throughout that week and beyond, resulting in a vision to form a community of entrepreneurs compelled by their faith to bring forth vital, dynamic human organizations that help heal our broken world.

The result – a coworking community + event space called Wayfare and a startup incubator for restorative movements called Wayfare Labs, a California non-profit corporation.

Program Details

The five month Launch program is intensive, where founders are guided through six major categories of startup development–strategy, business model, communication, financials, leadership, and product/program–as they launch or relaunch their ideas. This program offers dedicated weekly coaching, founders dinners, and workshops with access to a robust advisor network and Wayfare workspace–all delivered in a community of support with other startup founders.

The Growth program–offered to those who finish our Launch program and other select startups–provides a customized package of Wayfare membership, coaching, workshops, resources, tools, networks, and emotional support to build their organization into scalability.

We’re on the hunt for those interested in founding new organizations and those able to provide financial support for their work.

FAQ

  1. What happens at Wayfare Labs?

    Wayfare Labs runs a startup incubator program for founders seeking to birth organizations that intentionally pursue the world’s best future. Founders will launch and/or refine their world-changing ideas into viability through a program that includes coaching, workshops, networking, partner development, a pitch night, and more.

  2. How much time per week should I expect?

    Startups require significant commitment. Through Wayfare’s unlimited membership package, you’ll have full access to work out of Wayfare’s collaborative workspace to develop your organization. Participation in the program events–dinners, workshops, and presentations will be scheduled in consistent time blocks each week.

  3. What happens at the dinners?

    We host guest founders around the dinner table each week to share off-the-record and engage in Q&A about the good, bad, and ugly experiences they’ve had through the launch of startups. You will also get time to process your experiences with fellow cohort members.

  4. What happens on presentations days?

    On presentation days each month, founders practice a comprehensive pitch of their startups to their cohort members, coaches, and advisors. As startups pilot, test, and solidify elements of their business models through the program, presentations will become well rounded, preparing founders for launch night, where they’ll formally pitch to impact investors, advisors, family members, and friends.

  5. What happens at the workshops?

    Workshops are meant to serve as a launching pad for new areas of exploration for your startup. They will guide your thinking to further develop your story and specific elements of your business model and operations.

  6. Do we need to have a business plan before we begin the program?

    We’re looking for founders with difference-making ideas who have begun to take those ideas into the marketplace, but it’s not necessary that they’ve taken steps to formalize all their organizational elements. We’ll help you land on a business model in order to grow and scale your organization.

  7. Can we be a part of the program without moving to where you are?

    A critical part of our mission is that founders going through our program are immersed in a community and encouraging and supporting one another. With that said, it would be required that participants are able to work from our collaborative workspace located in Costa Mesa, CA.

  8. How much does it cost?

    Program fees are $10,000 for the first phase (the Launch phase), and we customize monthly packages for the Growth phase, which includes options for Wayfare coworking membership, advisor sessions, workshops, coaching, access to alumni network, and networking events. Collaborative fundraising tools and campaigns will be provided to assist in raising these program fees.

  9. How can we get funding for our startup?

    We will help you with resources and ideas about methods of fundraising, and then the rest is up to you! Fundraising and networking with potential donors is a skill needed for any organization to be successful, so we believe this will be a great opportunity to practice.
